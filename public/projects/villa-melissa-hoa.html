About Us

A Community Built on Comfort & Affordability

Villa Melissa Subdivision is a proud residential community located in Sitio Curva, Barangay Yati, Liloan, Cebu — one of the fastest-growing municipalities in Metro Cebu. Developed by Kenrich Development, our subdivision offers quality, affordable homes designed for Filipino families who dream of owning a decent, comfortable space to call their own.

Our Homeowners Association (HOA) is dedicated to upholding the peace, safety, and well-being of every resident. We work together to maintain our community's standards, organize events, and ensure that Villa Melissa remains a great place to live and grow.

Living Together Well

Community Rules & Guidelines

Our community rules exist to protect the rights and well-being of every resident. By following these guidelines, we collectively ensure that Villa Melissa remains a peaceful, clean, and harmonious place to call home.

Failure to comply with HOA rules may result in written notices, fines, or other actions as deemed appropriate by the Board of Trustees. Residents are encouraged to report concerns to the HOA office.

  • Rule 01 — HOA Dues Monthly HOA dues must be paid on or before the 5th of each month. Late payment is subject to a penalty fee as determined by the Board.
  • Rule 02 — Noise & Quiet Hours Loud music, parties, or activities that disturb neighbors are prohibited between 10:00 PM and 7:00 AM daily.
  • Rule 03 — Garbage Disposal Segregate biodegradable and non-biodegradable waste. Garbage must be placed in designated collection areas only on scheduled pickup days.
  • Rule 04 — Renovation & Construction All home renovation and construction activities require prior written approval from the HOA. Work is only permitted Monday–Saturday, 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM.
  • Rule 05 — Pets Pets must be kept within the property or on a leash when in common areas. Owners are responsible for cleaning up after their animals.
  • Rule 06 — Vehicle Parking Vehicles must park only in designated spaces. Parking on the main road or common areas that obstructs traffic flow is strictly prohibited.
  • Rule 07 — Visitors & Guests Residents are responsible for the conduct of their visitors. Guests must register with the HOA for overnight stays exceeding 3 days.
  • Rule 08 — Community Cleanliness Every homeowner is responsible for maintaining cleanliness in and around their property, including the sidewalk fronting their unit.

Our Town

Life in Liloan, Cebu

Known as the "Light of the North," Liloan is a first-class municipality in Metro Cebu with a population of over 160,000. Rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, Liloan is consistently recognized as one of the best-governed municipalities in the Philippines.

Historic Bagacay Lighthouse

Built in 1857 by the Spaniards and reconstructed in 1904, the Bagacay Point Lighthouse stands 22 meters tall and was declared a National Historical Landmark in 2004. It remains active today using solar energy.

Rosquillos Festival

Every May, Liloan celebrates the Rosquillos Festival honoring patron saint San Fernando El Rey. The town is famous for Titay's Rosquillos — delicate ring-shaped biscuits baked since 1907 that have become an iconic Cebuano pasalubong.

Adventure at Silot Bay

Papa Kit's Marina & Fishing Lagoon at Silot Bay offers kayaking, wakeboarding, zipline, wall climbing, and a floating restaurant — all within easy reach of Villa Melissa.

Award-Winning Governance

In 2019, Liloan was awarded Top Model Municipality in the Philippines. It was also recognized as the top Education Hub, top Retirement Hub, and Most Resilient Municipality for 2019 and 2020.

Heritage & Culture

The San Fernando El Rey Parish is one of the oldest churches in Cebu, dating to the 1840s. Uniquely, it is the only Catholic church in Cebu that faces west — a design intended to shield morning worshippers from the sun.

Pier 88 — Gateway North

Inaugurated in May 2023, Pier 88 connects Liloan to Camotes Islands, Lapu-Lapu City, and Cebu City — strengthening the town's role as a vital transport and commerce hub in Central Visayas.
